Gimnasio USM lets you book training blocks, track your fitness profile, and access your workout routines — all from one platform. This guide walks you through creating your account and making your first reservation.

<Note>
  Your role (student, coach, or admin) is assigned by an administrator after registration. New accounts default to the student role until updated.
</Note>

## Steps to get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Register with your RUT">
    Go to `/registro` and fill in your details. The registration form collects the following fields:

    | Field | Notes |
    |-------|-------|
    | **RUT** | Chilean national ID, auto-formatted as you type (e.g. `12345678-9`) |
    | **Nombres** | Your first name(s) |
    | **Apellido Paterno** | Your paternal surname |
    | **Apellido Materno** | Your maternal surname |
    | **Carrera** | Your academic programme (e.g. Ingeniería en Informática) |
    | **Contraseña** | Minimum 8 characters |
    | **Confirmar Contraseña** | Must match the password field |

    <Tip>
      The RUT field strips any non-numeric characters automatically and inserts the hyphen before the verification digit as you type. You do not need to format it manually — just type the digits and the `K` if applicable.
    </Tip>

    Once submitted, the form posts to `usuarios/registro/` on the backend. If the RUT is already registered, you will see an error message.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Log in">
    Go to `/login` and enter your credentials.

    ```text Login form fields
    RUT o Usuario  →  e.g. 12345678-9
    Contraseña     →  your password
    ```

    The RUT field on the login page also formats your input automatically as you type. The app sends your credentials to the `token/` endpoint and stores the returned JWT tokens in `localStorage` under the keys `access_token` and `refresh_token`.

    <Note>
      All subsequent API requests are authenticated automatically. The Axios instance attaches the `access_token` as a `Bearer` header on every request.
    </Note>

    After a successful login you are redirected to `/`, the home page, where available training blocks are displayed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Browse available training blocks">
    The home page (`/`) shows all upcoming training blocks grouped by date. Each block card displays:

    - The block code and time slot (e.g. `07:00 - 08:30`)
    - The number of available spots (`cupos_disponibles`)
    - The assigned coach's name

    Click a date header to expand its blocks. The first date with available blocks is expanded automatically when the page loads.

    <Warning>
      If an administrator has closed the gym, you will see a "Gimnasio Cerrado" screen instead of the booking grid. No reservations can be made while the gym is closed.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Make your first reservation">
    Click **Reservar** on any block card with available spots. The app posts to `reservas/` with the block ID and date.

    If a block is fully booked (`cupos_disponibles === 0`) and you hold the `es_seleccionado` flag, a **Reservar (Sobrecupo)** button appears, letting you join the waitlist beyond the normal capacity limit. Students without this flag see an **Agotado** (fully booked) state instead.

    After a successful reservation, the block list reloads automatically to reflect the updated spot count.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Role-based navigation

After login, the navbar adapts to your role. The platform uses three roles, identified by the `rol` field returned from `usuarios/me/`:

| Role | Value | Navbar links |
|------|-------|--------------|
| **Alumno** (student) | `1` | Inicio, Mis Reservas, Mi Ficha, Mis Rutinas |
| **Entrenador** (coach) | `2` | Inicio, Asistencia, Gestor de Rutinas, Gestión Horarios, Fichas Alumnos |
| **Admin** | `3` | Inicio, Asistencia, Gestión Horarios, Gestión Usuarios |

<Note>
  Route access is enforced on the server side via the `ProtectedRoute` component, which calls `usuarios/me/` on every navigation to verify your role before rendering the page. If your token is expired or your role does not match the required roles for a route, you are redirected to the home page.
</Note>

## Backend connection

The frontend connects to the Django backend using a shared Axios instance. The default base URL is:

```text Default API base URL
http://localhost:8000/api/
```

<Note>
  To point the frontend at a different backend (for example, a staging server), update the `baseURL` value in `src/api/axios.js`.
</Note>
